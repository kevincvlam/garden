  1. Know Where All the Notes Are on the Guitar

  2. Suggested practice, play all 12 notes in all possible positions to a metronome, in no particular order

  3. Guitar is Designed to Fall; So Are You

    1. Avoid holding up the guitar, let it fall naturally, and let your body fall naturally

  4. Center Your Register

    1. Don’t hang around in the same high e string range all the time
    2. Be aware of different ranges, how do singers sound? what’s your vocal range?

  5. You’re Worth Listening To

    1. Allaudin Mathieu’s lesson
      1. Practice improvising 1-minute long compositions and record them
      2. Do a minimum of ten per day for 7 days without listening to them until the very end
      3. After a week listen back to the recordings (70 minutes straight)
      4. Take notes about trends and what you want to do more or less of!

  6. Once You Play A Note, It’s Too Late

    1. Don’t dwell on what you’ve already played, good or bad, its gone
      1. No vicious cycles of dwelling on bad notes

  7. Two Things That Arent Reserved for Advanced Guitarists: Counterpoint and Playing Solo Guitar

    1. Jimmy Wyble’s book The Art Of Two-Line improvisation
    2. Solo Guitar, do it!

  8. throw Away Your Tuner for a Month

    1. Use a reference pitch instead
    2. brings intonation into the picture and you learn your own tendencies
